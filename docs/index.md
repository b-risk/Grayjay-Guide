# Grayjay Guide

This is a comprehensive guide to getting the best experience with Grayjay, which covers topics from client usage to YouTube blocks workarounds, and more to be planned.

# Introduction to Grayjay

Grayjay is an aggregated media client developed by FUTO for following and supporting creators directly, rather than the platform. Grayjay is *not a YouTube client,* as the goal of the project is much broader, and even if YouTube were to go away completely Grayjay would still not be useless. Unlike other clients as well, Grayjay has a full-time developer team behind it dedicated to improving the client and fixing the built-in plugins as their sites are constantly changing, this is one of the reasons that Grayjay is a paid product.

## What are Plugins?

Grayjay uses a plugin system to allow you to follow creators on multiple platforms, these plugins do not implement advertisements and you are expected to support creators you find valuable directly, the majority of plugins also do not use official APIs and FUTO is not partnered with any company, therefore everything that Grayjay does is reverse engineering. Infact, back in 2024 FUTO was given a cease and desist by Google saying to stop using their API and stop all development imeddiately on Grayjay, they responded by simply stating they do not use their API and their developers have not agreed to their terms, and ever since Google has been removing videos off of YouTube even mentioning the project.

## Not Relying on Just One Platform

One of the reasons Grayjay exists is to allow users not have to rely on just one platform to follow a creator on, making it anti-censorship and keeping a direct connection between the user and content creator. As we use Grayjay, we should get used to following and supporting creators on multiple platforms, and in this guide I hope to make the transition as easy as possible. If your creator provides content elsewhere, follow them there, if it's a paid platform like Patreon then you should support them there as well, if they don't get any support from users then they have no incentive to post their content on alternative platforms.
